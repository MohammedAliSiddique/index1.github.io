Abdur Rehman Naqash

CAMEL SKIN PRODUCTS
Malik Abdul Rehman Naqash's family has been involved in the art of “Naqashi” for nine centuries. His grandfather, Ustad Abdullah Naqash, made first camel skin lamp in 1910 and clayman Ustad Hayat Muhammad made first mould of the lamp that gave a new life and spirit to the camel skin art as well as Naqashi. After Abdullah Naqash, his son Ashiq Naqash(Malik abdul rehman's father) gave new touches to the art of his forefathers by working on pots, articles of wood like furniture, doors, cots, jewellery boxes and stands beside walls and roofs of famous buildings. Malik Abdul Rehman Naqash learnt this art from his father and now he is conducting workshops on camel skin craft across the world including India, USA and Germany.

Abdul Majeed Soomro

BLOCK PRINTED AJRAK
Abdul Majeed Soomro belongs to Bhit Shah , Sindh. Making Ajrak is their Family Heirloom. Abdul Majeed Soomro learnt this work from his father. He makes ajrak colthes, ajrak shawls, ajrak bedsheets etc. People demand his ajraks worldwide. Altough Abdul Majeed Soomro wants to sell his products worldwide but he has no facilities to send those products to people of different countries.

Ameeran Bibi

HANDMADE RILLI
Ameeran Bibi is from Sindh, Pakistan. Ameeran Bibi has perfected the craft of making Rilli over the years. She sells her products directly to customers in and outside Pakistan from her village in Sukkur.

Hadia Sohail

Embellishments
Embellishments was founded four years ago by Hadia Sohail who herself was passionate about wearing wasual jewelry. Unable to find affordable and casual digital lockets and ethnic jewelry, she came up with the idea to design the jewelry for daily wear. Despite having zero knowledge of jewelry design, she did research, collected designs, contacted vendors and managed to come up with unique jewelry.

About Us

"Dreamweaver" is a social enterprise which strives to empower artists and artisans. Dreamweaver aims to connect the unconnected artisans to the local and international markets by not only providing them with a market place but also marketing them in an effective way. Dreamweaver works for the revival of the dying crafts by fusing them with modern and marketable designs and by giving design consultation to the artisans. We, at Dreamweaver, consider artisans as one big family and reach out to them wherever they need us.
